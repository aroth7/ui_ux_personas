The Notorious CVS Checkout Machine

Sketch and Description

This interface is the CVS self-checkout machine. It is trying to solve the problem of every customer having to wait in line for the in person checkout station, which usually only has 1-2 employees, as well as speed up the checkout process in general. To use it, one scans ites in front of the scanner, then places the scanned items on the gray bagging table. While doing this, the user interacts with many buttons on the red screen.

Observations and Interview Repsonses

Observations
  • multiple users forgot to press “skip bagging” after scanning an item
  • 2 of the 4 users had to call for help at some point
  • 2 of the 4 users tried to pay before pressing all of the buttons to initiate the payment process
  • 1 user almost left without pressing “no receipt”
  • there was decently sized line of 3-4 other people waiting to use self-checkout, while there was no line for in-person checkout
  • all of the users looked frustrated at some point during their checkout process
Questions
1. Describe your experience using the self-checkout machine in 2 words
  • "nothing special"
  • "efficient and non-eventful"
  • "frustrated and annoyed"
2. Do you think using self checkout speeds up or slows down your checkout process?
  • "slows it down because I sometimes forget to press some of the buttons to continue"
  • "Speeds it up because you don’t have to wait for everyone on line for traditional checkout"
  • frustrated and annoyed
3. Approximately how long did it take you to use the machine?
  • "Less than 1.5 mins"
  • "30 seconds"
4. Why did you choose self checkout over traditional checkout?
  • "I don’t like talking to people"
  • "I only had one item, so it wasn’t worth the wait for traditional checkout"
5. Would you choose self checkout again?
  • "yes"
  • "yes"

Personas

Stressed Sally is a college student. She is rushing between classes and is trying to buy a diet coke and a protein bar between classes. Because Sally is stressed, she forgets to press the buttons in the correct order, which causes the machine to glitch. Additionally, the scanner malfunctions while Sally is using the machine, so she has to call the actual checkout person over for help. The Stressed Sally persona represents users who get confused by all the different steps one must follow, as well as the group of users who have to deal with the machine not working because she dealt with both of these issues while checking out.

Efficient Edward is a businessman who often has to stop at CVS on the way home to pick up one or two items. He doesn’t personally face any problems with the interface; however, he does have to wait longer than expected while the people in front of him struggle. The Efficient Edward persona represents the group of users who use the machine effectively to purchase only one item and also don’t run into any issues regarding the machine breaking.
